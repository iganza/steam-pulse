* SteamPulse

** Operating Criterion — Asynchronous Transactions

   Every surface, tier, or feature must support the full transaction
   flow asynchronously: a buyer can discover, preview, pay, and
   receive value without operator real-time involvement. Stripe
   Checkout, email-delivered unlock links, recurring subscriptions,
   and scheduled email all qualify. Live sales calls, custom-quoted
   proposals, and "hop on a call before you can buy" flows do not.

   SteamPulse is subscription-primary with a per-genre one-time
   alternative, fronted by a free per-game funnel. The primary SKU
   is a $19/mo or $190/yr subscription that unlocks every published
   genre report (current and future) plus all editorial revisions.
   Buyers who only want one specific genre can buy a single report
   one-time at $79. Per-game pages stay free forever: a showcase
   set (benchmark games of published genre reports) renders the
   full per-game report; every other analyzed game renders an
   abbreviated preview pointing at the relevant genre report. The
   canonical monetization direction lives in
   =scripts/prompts/monetization-direction.md=. SKUs beyond
   subscription + one-time genre report are listed under "Deferred"
   below.

** Executive Summary

   SteamPulse turns Steam reviews and store metadata into structured
   intelligence at two levels: per-game (commodity floor, free) and
   per-genre (the paid SKU). The artifact a buyer pays for is
   "Promise Gaps across the top 10 Soulslikes," not "one game's
   analysis." Per-game pages exist to drive SEO discovery, prove the
   depth of the underlying analysis, and upsell into the relevant
   genre report.

   The three-phase LLM pipeline (chunk, merge, synthesise) produces
   per-game =GameReport= rows that feed the free per-game pages.
   =mv_genre_synthesis= aggregates the per-game data into the
   cohort-level synthesis that ships as the genre report. The Phase
   4 synthesis output is the v1 of each genre report; editorial
   polish is iterative post-launch (no editorial pre-launch block).

   - *Primary SKU:* $19/mo or $190/yr subscription. Unlocks all
     published genre reports plus every future report and revision
     while the subscription is active. Aligns with industry anchor
     (GameDiscoverCo $15/mo, VGInsights $20/mo).
   - *Alternative SKU:* $79 one-time per genre report. For buyers
     who only want one specific genre. Test starting price; iterate
     based on conversion (raise to $99 if > 4%, drop or remove if
     < 1%).
   - *Surface:* every per-game page is free. Showcase games render
     the full per-game report (no email gate, no teaser bar).
     Standard pages render the abbreviated preview with one upsell
     line pointing at the relevant genre report.
   - *Production:* v1 of each genre report = auto-generated Phase 4
     synthesis output, formatted as a PDF with cover, table of
     contents, methodology footer. Editorial polish (sequencing,
     framing, executive summary, charts) is iterative post-launch;
     subscribers receive every revision automatically.
   - *Cadence:* target one new genre report every 6 to 8 weeks.
     Faster than quarterly because subscription value scales with
     catalog volume. The Phase 4 synthesiser unlocks this cadence
     since the marginal cost of a new report is the LLM run plus a
     few hours of formatting.
   - *Add-on micro-tools ($9 to $29):* Tag Doctor, Page Doctor,
     Niche Scout, Concept Doctor. Sell standalone or bundle as a
     "free with subscription" perk.
   - *Operator labour:* ~5 to 10 hours/month steady state.
     Editorial polish on existing reports + 1 new report every
     6 to 8 weeks (~5 to 10 hours of formatting per new report).

** Project Description

   Steam hosts over 100,000 games with millions of player reviews,
   but this qualitative data is not surfaced as cross-cohort
   structured intelligence. Per-game summaries are commodity
   (Datahumble, LEYWARE, Sentimeter, HowlRound, SteamReview AI,
   GG Insights all produce single-pass surface summaries).
   *Frequency-weighted cross-genre synthesis with quote citations*
   is whitespace.

   The three-phase pipeline:

   1. *Chunk* batches 50 reviews at a time into structured
      TopicSignal extractions across 9 categories (design_praise,
      gameplay_friction, wishlist_items, dropout_moments,
      technical_issues, refund_signals, community_health,
      monetization_sentiment, content_depth).
   2. *Merge* collapses per-chunk signals into a per-game
      =GameReport= with quote-traceable claims and anti-duplication
      enforcement. Per-game =GameReport= rows feed the free per-game
      pages.
   3. *Synthesise* aggregates =N= per-game reports in a niche into a
      single =mv_genre_synthesis= row. This Phase 4 output is the
      v1 of each genre report (the LLM synthesis on 141 RDB games
      tested as "truly gold" out of the pipeline). Editorial polish
      (sequencing, framing, executive summary, charts, cross-
      references) is iterative post-launch; subscribers receive
      every revision automatically.

   The buyable artifact is the genre report. Per-game =GameReport=
   data ships free as the funnel and proof-of-depth: showcase games
   (benchmark games of published genre reports) render their full
   per-game data; every other analyzed game renders an abbreviated
   preview (top 3 strengths, top 3 complaints, basic metadata) with
   an upsell line pointing at the relevant genre report.

** Wedge Strategy: ship the Roguelike Deckbuilder genre report first

   The launch artifact is the RDB genre report, shipped as v1 from
   the auto-generated Phase 4 synthesis. The launch work is Stripe
   subscription plumbing + per-game preview frontend + PDF
   formatting of the existing =mv_genre_synthesis= output. No new
   LLM analysis required. No editorial pre-launch block.

*** Why RDB is the right launch wedge
    - Phase 4 synthesis has already run on 141 RDB games. The
      =mv_genre_synthesis= row is live and the user has confirmed
      the output is "truly gold."
    - 145 eligible games at ≥ 500 reviews, 87.5% avg positive
      sentiment. Engaged audience writes richer reviews; richer
      reviews produce sharper synthesis.
    - Clear benchmark games (Slay the Spire, Balatro, Inscryption,
      Monster Train, Dicey Dungeons) for the showcase set.
    - Reviewers write analytically about systems (run length, build
      variety, RNG fairness, meta-progression). Ideal input for
      cross-game synthesis.

    Once the RDB genre report ships, these 5 games' per-game pages
    render the full =GameReport= visible (no preview gating, no
    email gate, no teaser bar):
    - Slay the Spire
    - Balatro
    - Inscryption
    - Monster Train
    - Dicey Dungeons

    Every other analyzed game (RDB or otherwise) renders the
    abbreviated preview. The showcase set grows by 3 to 5 games
    every time a new genre report ships.

*** Cost expectations per genre report
    - Analysis run (~145 games at $0.50 to $1.00 each): $70 to $145
      (sunk cost for RDB; budgeted per future genre)
    - Phase 4 synthesiser refresh: ~$5/month per niche
    - PDF formatting and design: ~5 to 10 hours per new report
    - Page serving: matview + report row read + CDN. Negligible.
    - Stripe fees per sale: 2.9% + $0.30 one-time, or recurring
      Stripe fees on subscription
    - Breakeven on a new report's sunk LLM cost: ~2 one-time sales
      at $79, or ~8 net-new monthly subscribers
    - Editorial polish: iterative post-launch, not pre-launch

*** Dev/Marketing Toolkit Brainstorm

    Brainstormed 2026-04-28 alongside Page Doctor v1; reorganised by
    lifecycle phase 2026-04-30 after Niche Scout v1, Concept Doctor v1
    and Tag Doctor v1 shipped. Anchor pattern for every entry: a dev or
    marketer types an appid (or genre/wedge slug) and gets a focused,
    peer-grounded report. All on-demand, never scheduled (except
    Competitor Watch). None is launch-blocking; surface as future
    prompts when demand fires.

    Status legend: [SHIPPED] live in =scripts/=, [WIP] active prompt in
    =scripts/prompts/=, [IDEA] not yet specced.

**** Phase 1: Discovery and momentum (pre-concept)
- [SHIPPED] *Niche Scout*: scans the catalog for fertile (genre, modifier_tag) niches by demand, growth, saturation, longtail, indie scope.
- [IDEA] *Trend Spotter*: time-axis sibling of Niche Scout. Surfaces tags or genres where 12mo demand is up >X% but releases-per-quarter is flat. =mv_tag_trend=, =mv_trend_by_tag=, =mv_trend_by_genre= already exist; mostly stitching.
- [IDEA] *Wedge Finder*: given two tags, is the crossover unfilled? Quick exploration cousin of Concept Doctor's differentiation section. Pairwise tag co-occurrence + revenue.

**** Phase 2: Decision support (pre-build, validation)
- [SHIPPED] *Concept Doctor*: GO / CONDITIONAL GO / NO verdict for a target concept, grounded in cohort tag DNA + per-peer Phase-3 GameReport signals.
- [IDEA] *Pricing Doctor*: price recommendation grounded in comparables, content hours from reviews, owner-tier estimates from Boxleiter. Uniquely defensible (reviews + owner estimates). Top-3 next.
- [IDEA] *Competitive Brief*: "what genre am I really in, and how am I distinct?" Leverages genre depth. POC prompt already drafted in =scripts/prompts/maybe/competitive-brief-poc.md=.
- [IDEA] *Pre-Launch Health Score*: for unreleased games. No GameReport possible for the dev's own game, so leans entirely on page quality + peer patterns. Direct fit for the "has Demo, weak wishlists" persona.

**** Phase 3: Asset micro-audits (pre-launch craft)
- [SHIPPED] *Tag Doctor*: tag-only audit. Drop generic tags, add the sub-genre tags peers ride. Run-monthly cadence.
- [WIP] *Page Doctor*: full store page audit. v1 and v2 specs in =scripts/prompts/=.
- [IDEA] *Capsule Doctor*: capsule-only deep dive. Vision-heavy. Word count, contrast, thumbnail readability, 1-second genre signal vs ~10 peer capsules. Blocked on vision budget.
- [IDEA] *Trailer Doctor*: first 5 seconds, opens-with-gameplay vs logo, sound-independence, length window, peer trailer patterns. Blocked on video ingestion.

**** Phase 4: Positioning and acquisition
- [IDEA] *Tagline Lab*: 10 short-description variants grounded in actual reviewer phrasing.
- [IDEA] *Wishlist Audience Targeter*: from review demographics, infer where wishlisters hang out (subreddits, streamers, content niches). Acquisition-channel output, not page output.
- [IDEA] *Localization Decider*: review distribution by language + peer localization ROI heuristics. "Translate to these N next, expected lift, cost."

**** Phase 5: Time-anchored (event-driven)
- [IDEA] *Launch Window Doctor*: given a planned release_date + tag-stack, count peers releasing within +/- 14 and +/- 30 days; surface quietest week in next 90. Pure SQL, no LLM needed.
- [IDEA] *Demo Diagnostic*: pulls demo-tagged and EA reviews. "Is the demo cannibalising wishlists or seeding them?" vs peer demo-to-wishlist patterns from Next Fest histories.
- [IDEA] *Next Fest Pre-Mortem*: 30-day countdown checklist anchored to last Next Fest's peer outcomes.

**** Phase 6: Live-game tools (post-launch optimisation)
- [IDEA] *Review Doctor*: post-launch corollary to Concept Doctor. Top 3-5 sprint-sized changes from the dev's own Phase-3 GameReport (friction_clusters, wishlist_items, dropout_moments), ranked by mention_count x severity. Reuses every Phase-3 report already paid for. Top-3 next.
- [IDEA] *Velocity Doctor*: review velocity vs peers at the same lifecycle stage. Cohort p25/p50/p75 over =review_velocity_lifetime= bucketed by age-since-launch.
- [IDEA] *Patch Notes Optimiser*: given a planned patch + GameReport.player_wishlist, what to highlight to re-engage lapsed wishlists.
- [IDEA] *Refund Risk Diagnostic*: package existing GameReport.churn_triggers + technical_issues as a standalone surface.
- [IDEA] *Review Reply Coach*: which negative reviews to respond to publicly, what to say. Devs underuse this lever.

**** Phase 7: Strategic and portfolio (cross-game, low volume audience)
- [IDEA] *Studio Doctor*: given a developer slug, audit the full catalogue for tag drift, niche fit, cannibalisation. Audience: serial indies and small studios.
- [IDEA] *Competitor Watch*: only scheduled tool in the family. Weekly delta on review_count, price_usd, positive_pct, top-5 tags for 3-5 closest peers of a chosen appid. Needs a small snapshot history table; could live in =index_insights= JSON.

**** Top 3 to build next (post Niche-Scout + Concept-Doctor + Tag-Doctor)
1. *Review Doctor*: closes the post-launch loop that Tag Doctor only half-covers; reuses every Phase-3 GameReport already in =reports.report_json=. Strongest "your players told us X" hook.
2. *Pricing Doctor*: uniquely defensible from review + owner-estimate data; pricing is high-stakes and devs guess. Pairs with Concept Doctor (pre-build) and Tag Doctor (post-launch).
3. *Trend Spotter*: matviews already exist; cheapest-to-build of the discovery-phase ideas; adds time-axis distinct from Niche Scout's snapshot view.

**** Blocked or parked (data gap)
- *Capsule Doctor*, *Trailer Doctor*: need vision/video.
- *Wishlist forecast*: Steamworks gated.
- *Patch cadence*: needs patch-note ingestion.
- *Audience Crosswalk* (what other niches do my players shop): needs behavioural data Steam does not expose.
