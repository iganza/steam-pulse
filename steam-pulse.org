* SteamPulse

** Operating Principle

   *"If work can't be built once and sold 1,000 times without my
   involvement, it doesn't belong in this business."*

   SteamPulse is a self-serve catalog of LLM-synthesised Steam genre
   research reports. The product sells and delivers value while the
   operator sleeps. Every future feature, tier, or surface must pass
   the sleep test — otherwise it is killed or gated behind a numerical
   revenue trigger. See "Tier 2 — Gated" below.

** Executive Summary

   SteamPulse produces deep, cited genre research reports for Steam
   indie devs, studios, and publishers. Each report synthesises the
   complete player-review corpus of a single niche (141 games for the
   launch wedge) into a cross-cutting narrative: friction points,
   wishlist items, benchmark games, churn timing, and ranked dev
   priorities — all quote-backed to individual reviews.

   The three-phase LLM pipeline (chunk → merge → synthesise) is the
   unfair advantage. Competitors do single-pass per-game analysis; no
   one else publishes frequency-weighted cross-game synthesis at
   narrative depth with citation traceability.

   - *Product:* catalog of flagship PDF reports, three self-serve tiers
     ($49 indie / $149 studio / $499 publisher). Stripe Checkout + S3
     signed-URL delivery. Zero operator touch per sale.
   - *Surface:* every per-game page and genre summary page is 100% free
     and indexed for SEO long-tail discovery. No content is gated
     anywhere. Reports are the only paid product.
   - *Cadence:* one new report per quarter. Reports never expire. The
     catalog compounds.
   - *Operator labour:* ~15 hours/month average — 40-60 hours per
     report (curation, design, editorial voice), spread across the
     quarter. Zero ongoing support, zero weekly content cadence, zero
     manual fulfilment.

** Project Description

   Steam hosts over 100,000 games with millions of player reviews, but
   this qualitative data remains largely untouched at the aggregate
   level. Per-game review summaries are commodity (Datahumble, LEYWARE,
   Sentimeter, HowlRound, SteamReview AI, GG Insights all produce this
   shape). *Cross-genre synthesis* — frequency-weighted aggregation of
   friction points, wishlist items, benchmark games, and churn timing
   across every game in a single niche — is whitespace.

   The three-phase pipeline:

   1. *Chunk* — batch 50 reviews at a time into structured TopicSignal
      extractions across 9 categories (design_praise, gameplay_friction,
      wishlist_items, dropout_moments, technical_issues, refund_signals,
      community_health, monetization_sentiment, content_depth).
   2. *Merge* — collapse per-chunk signals into a per-game =GameReport=
      with quote-traceable claims and anti-duplication enforcement.
   3. *Synthesise* — aggregate =N= per-game reports in a niche into a
      single =mv_genre_synthesis= row with mention_count ≥ 3
      frequency-weighted friction clusters, auto-detected benchmark
      games, median dropout-hour timing, and ranked cross-genre dev
      priorities.

   The LLM output is a structured first draft. The published *report*
   is that draft plus 40-60 hours of human editorial craft: framing,
   sequencing, methodology transparency, executive summary, visual
   design. The craft layer is what a buyer pays for; the pipeline is
   what makes the craft tractable.

** Wedge Strategy: Roguelike Deckbuilder Deep Coverage

   Decision date: 2026-04-14. Depth-in-niche strategy; 141 eligible
   games (≥ 500 reviews each), ~$70-141 total LLM cost for whole-niche
   coverage.

*** Why roguelike-deckbuilder
    From catalog query 2026-04-14 (eligibility: ≥ 500 reviews):

    | tag                   | eligible_games | avg_reviews | avg_positive_pct |
    |-----------------------+----------------+-------------+------------------|
    | roguelike             |           1001 |        8000 |             83.6 |
    | roguelite             |            925 |        8231 |             83.8 |
    | deckbuilding          |            334 |        6700 |             83.5 |
    | roguelike-deckbuilder |            141 |        6590 |             87.5 |

    - *141 games = "complete coverage" affordable*: ~$70-141 total at
      ~$0.50-$1.00/game. Whole niche, not a sample.
    - *87.5% avg positive sentiment* — engaged audience writes richer
      reviews → richer synthesis.
    - *Reviewers write analytically about systems* (run length, build
      variety, RNG fairness, meta-progression) — ideal input for
      cross-game synthesis.
    - *Clear benchmark games* (Slay the Spire, Balatro, Inscryption,
      Monster Train, Dicey Dungeons) for cross-linking.

    Broader Roguelike (1001 games) is the expansion target *if* the
    wedge demo lands.

*** Cost expectations
    - Analysis run (141 games): $70-141 worst case
    - Cross-genre synthesizer (one row, weekly refresh): ~$5/month
    - Page serving: matview row read + CDN. Negligible.
    - *Total to launch and operate Report #1: under $200 in compute +
      ~60 hours of editorial curation.*

    artifact and ships first. Pre-orders for the paid PDF open next,
    providing the revenue signal that justifies the 60 hours of
    editorial work. The polished PDF ships last, delivered
    automatically to pre-order buyers when =published_at= reaches now.
    Free per-game + free genre pages for SEO throughout. One new
    report per quarter once the catalog is operational. Nothing else.
