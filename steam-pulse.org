** Why this order

   Build distribution before the paywall. Every gaming-industry
   intelligence comparable that succeeded at this price point
   (GameDiscoverCo Plus, MDM Pro, Naavik) has a free newsletter
   as the acquisition channel. Without it, CAC for a $19/mo SaaS
   is ~$50-200 from paid ads, upside-down on a 3-month median
   consumer-SaaS retention. The newsletter compounds for free,
   but only if the operator commits to weekly cadence for 6-12
   months. The paid surface engineering can wait; the audience
   cannot.

   Phase D's concierge step (manual reports for $79) replaces
   the previous "ship Stripe Checkout first" plan because: (a)
   it validates pricing and ICP without 1-2 days of auth +
   checkout engineering, (b) repeat purchase rate <20% would
   invalidate the model regardless of how good the checkout
   is, (c) the same buyers become the founding members of the
   subscription tier when Phase E ships.

** Operating criterion: asynchronous transactions

   Every paid surface, once shipped, must support the full
   transaction flow asynchronously: a buyer can discover,
   preview, pay, and receive value without operator real-time
   involvement. Stripe Checkout, magic-link unlock emails,
   recurring subscriptions, and scheduled email all qualify.
   Live sales calls, custom-quoted proposals, and "hop on a
   call before you can buy" flows do not.

   Phase D's concierge step is the deliberate temporary
   exception: the operator runs the Phase 3 pipeline by hand
   to learn what buyers want before automating. Concierge ends
   at Phase E.

** Project description

   Steam hosts over 100,000 games with millions of player
   reviews, but this qualitative data is not surfaced as
   cross-cohort structured intelligence. Per-game summaries are
   commodity (Datahumble, LEYWARE, Sentimeter, HowlRound,
   SteamReview AI, GG Insights all produce single-pass surface
   summaries). *Frequency-weighted cross-genre synthesis with
   quote citations* is whitespace.

   The three-phase pipeline:

   1. *Chunk* batches 50 reviews at a time into structured
      TopicSignal extractions across 9 categories
      (=design_praise=, =gameplay_friction=, =wishlist_items=,
      =dropout_moments=, =technical_issues=, =refund_signals=,
      =community_health=, =monetization_sentiment=,
      =content_depth=).
   2. *Merge* collapses per-chunk signals into a per-game
      =GameReport= with quote-traceable claims and
      anti-duplication enforcement. Per-game =GameReport= rows
      feed the free per-game pages.
   3. *Synthesise* aggregates =N= per-game reports in a niche
      into a single =mv_genre_synthesis= row. This Phase 4
      output is the v1 of each genre report. Editorial polish
      (sequencing, framing, executive summary, charts,
      cross-references) is iterative post-launch; subscribers
      receive every revision automatically.

   The buyable artifact is the genre report. Per-game
   =GameReport= data ships free as the funnel and proof-of-depth.

** Wedge strategy: RDB ships first

   The launch artifact is the RDB genre report, shipped as v1
   from the auto-generated Phase 4 synthesis on 141
   roguelike-deckbuilder games. The RDB synthesis already
   exists and was confirmed "truly gold" out of the pipeline.

   Showcase set (full =GameReport= visible, free, never
   downgraded):
   - Slay the Spire
   - Balatro
   - Inscryption
   - Monster Train
   - Dicey Dungeons

   Why RDB:
   - Phase 4 synthesis already run; no new LLM cost.
   - 145 eligible games at ≥ 500 reviews, 87.5% avg positive
     sentiment. Engaged audience writes richer reviews; richer
     reviews produce sharper synthesis.
   - Reviewers write analytically about systems (run length,
     build variety, RNG fairness, meta-progression). Ideal
     input for cross-game synthesis.
   - Clear benchmark games for the showcase set.

   The showcase set grows by 3-5 games every time a new genre
   report ships, via the =benchmark_appids= rule.

** Cost summary

   *Per genre report:*
   - LLM analysis (~145 games at $0.50 to $1.00): $70-145
     (sunk for RDB; budgeted per future genre)
   - Phase 4 synthesiser refresh: ~$5/month per niche
   - PDF formatting and design: ~5-10 hours per new report
   - Editorial polish: iterative post-launch, no fixed budget

   *Recurring services:*
   - Substack: free until paid newsletter, then 10% of paid revenue
   - Resend free tier (10k/mo) sufficient at launch volumes
   - Plausible $9/mo
   - Stripe fees: 2.9% + $0.30 per sale or per subscription invoice
   - S3 storage and CDN: negligible

   *Breakeven on a new report's sunk LLM cost:* ~2 one-time
   sales at $79, or ~8 net-new monthly subscribers.

** Marketing copy

   Rule: never say "AI" in headlines or CTAs. Show the output,
   not the tech.

*** Homepage hero
    - *Primary:* "Know your genre before you ship." /
      "Cross-cohort player intelligence for Steam game devs.
      Every review of every competitor in a niche, distilled
      into structured findings. Free weekly newsletter; full
      reports $19/mo or $79 each."
    - *Alt (dev-targeted):* "Read every review of every
      competitor, without reading every review."

*** Newsletter signup CTA (homepage + showcase pages)
    - "Subscribe to weekly roguelike-deckbuilder intelligence."
    - One email field, no name. No overlay. No exit-intent.

*** Genre report page (=/genre/[slug]/=) copy (post Phase E)
    - Lead with the single most surprising finding from the
      published report.
    - Show the full table of contents.
    - Public 5-page sample link (no email capture).
    - Two CTAs side by side: "Subscribe to all reports / $19/mo
      or $190/yr" and "Buy this report only / $79".
    - Frame as "the patterns across the cohort, with the
      receipts," not as "free vs paid feature list".

*** Per-game page copy (preview surface)
    - Top 3 strengths, top 3 complaints, basic metadata: present
      as summary, not as "preview" with a teaser bar.
    - Bottom of page: "See [Game] in the context of the [Genre]
      report. Subscribe $19/mo, or buy this report only $79."
    - For showcase games (full-visible): no upsell line; link in
      methodology footer to "see how this fits the [Genre]
      report".

*** Phrasing to avoid
    - "AI-synthesised player intelligence" → "Player
      intelligence distilled from thousands of reviews"
    - "AI-powered review analysis" → "Every review read, every
      pattern surfaced"
