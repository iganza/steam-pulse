# Questions a SteamPulse genre report answers for an indie dev

The paid genre report + per-game deep-dives are designed to answer the
concrete questions a solo or small-studio developer actually has when
deciding what to build and how. Drafted 2026-04-17 as a reference for
marketing copy, landing-page structure, and report table-of-contents
design.

## Genre / game selection

1. Is this genre healthy right now (sentiment, review velocity)?
2. Can I personally build the top 5 wishlist items players want?
3. Is the #1 friction solvable by a specific design choice I'd make?
4. Can I hit the benchmark games' quality bar on my budget?
5. Is there an underserved tonal niche (audience archetype or vibe no benchmark covers)?
6. What price point do the top games cluster at, and does my scope fit?

## Feature / design decisions

7. What are the top 10 friction points I must NOT repeat?
8. What are the top 5 wishlist items I must deliver (table stakes)?
9. When and why do players drop off, and what retention mechanic counters it?
10. What's the ranked dev priority list (frequency × impact) for this genre?
11. Which player archetype am I building for, and who am I explicitly NOT building for?
12. What store-page promises do competitors consistently break (and I should keep)?
13. What hidden strengths do players praise that marketing never mentions?

## Benchmark game lessons

14. What's the median run/session length in this genre, and why?
15. What monetization patterns (price, DLC, EA) does the audience tolerate vs resent?
16. What community norms define this genre (strategy sharing, modding, speedrunning, etc.)?
17. What does each benchmark game do well that I should steal, and what do players wish it did differently?

## Positioning / marketing

18. What Steam tags correlate with my target audience?
19. What's the single most-requested missing feature in the genre (= headline of my store page)?
20. Who are my 3 nearest competitors and where do their reviews concentrate complaints?
