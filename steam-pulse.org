* SteamPulse

** Operating Criterion — Asynchronous Transactions

   Every surface, tier, or feature must support the full transaction
   flow asynchronously: a buyer can discover, sign up, pay, and
   receive value without operator real-time involvement. Stripe
   Checkout, S3 signed-URL delivery, recurring subscriptions, and
   scheduled email all qualify. Live sales calls, custom-quoted
   proposals, and any "hop on a call before you can buy" flow do not.

   SteamPulse is a self-serve catalog of LLM-synthesised Steam genre
   research reports. New surfaces beyond the catalog are gated behind
   numerical triggers under "Tier 2 — Gated" below.

** Executive Summary

   SteamPulse produces deep, cited genre research reports for Steam
   indie devs, studios, and publishers. Each report synthesises the
   complete player-review corpus of a single niche (145 games for the
   launch wedge) into a cross-cutting narrative: friction points,
   wishlist items, benchmark games, churn timing, and ranked dev
   priorities — all quote-backed to individual reviews.

   The three-phase LLM pipeline (chunk → merge → synthesise) is the
   unfair advantage. Competitors do single-pass per-game analysis; no
   one else publishes frequency-weighted cross-game synthesis at
   narrative depth with citation traceability.

   - *Product:* catalog of flagship PDF reports, three self-serve tiers
     ($49 indie / $149 studio / $499 publisher). Stripe Checkout + S3
     signed-URL delivery. Zero operator touch per sale.
   - *Surface:* every per-game page and genre summary page is 100% free
     and indexed for SEO long-tail discovery. No content is gated
     anywhere. Reports are the only paid product.
   - *Cadence:* one new report per quarter. Reports never expire. The
     catalog compounds.
   - *Operator labour:* ~15 hours/month average — 40-60 hours per
     report (curation, design, editorial voice), spread across the
     quarter. Zero ongoing support, zero weekly content cadence, zero
     manual fulfilment.

** Project Description

   Steam hosts over 100,000 games with millions of player reviews, but
   this qualitative data remains largely untouched at the aggregate
   level. Per-game review summaries are commodity (Datahumble, LEYWARE,
   Sentimeter, HowlRound, SteamReview AI, GG Insights all produce this
   shape). *Cross-genre synthesis* — frequency-weighted aggregation of
   friction points, wishlist items, benchmark games, and churn timing
   across every game in a single niche — is whitespace.

   The three-phase pipeline:

   1. *Chunk* — batch 50 reviews at a time into structured TopicSignal
      extractions across 9 categories (design_praise, gameplay_friction,
      wishlist_items, dropout_moments, technical_issues, refund_signals,
      community_health, monetization_sentiment, content_depth).
   2. *Merge* — collapse per-chunk signals into a per-game =GameReport=
      with quote-traceable claims and anti-duplication enforcement.
   3. *Synthesise* — aggregate =N= per-game reports in a niche into a
      single =mv_genre_synthesis= row with mention_count ≥ 3
      frequency-weighted friction clusters, auto-detected benchmark
      games, median dropout-hour timing, and ranked cross-genre dev
      priorities.

   The LLM output is a structured first draft. The published *report*
   is that draft plus 40-60 hours of human editorial craft: framing,
   sequencing, methodology transparency, executive summary, visual
   design. The craft layer is what a buyer pays for; the pipeline is
   what makes the craft tractable.

** Wedge Strategy: Roguelike Deckbuilder Deep Coverage

   Depth-in-niche strategy; **145 eligible games** (≥ 500 reviews
   each), ~$70-145 total LLM cost for whole-niche coverage.

*** Why roguelike-deckbuilder
    From catalog query (eligibility: ≥ 500 reviews):

    | tag                   | eligible_games | avg_reviews | avg_positive_pct |
    |-----------------------+----------------+-------------+------------------|
    | roguelike             |           1001 |        8000 |             83.6 |
    | roguelite             |            925 |        8231 |             83.8 |
    | deckbuilding          |            334 |        6700 |             83.5 |
    | roguelike-deckbuilder |            145 |        6590 |             87.5 |

    - *145 games = "complete coverage" affordable*: ~$70-145 total at
      ~$0.50-$1.00/game. Whole niche, not a sample.
    - *87.5% avg positive sentiment* — engaged audience writes richer
      reviews → richer synthesis.
    - *Reviewers write analytically about systems* (run length, build
      variety, RNG fairness, meta-progression) — ideal input for
      cross-game synthesis.
    - *Clear benchmark games* (Slay the Spire, Balatro, Inscryption,
      Monster Train, Dicey Dungeons) for cross-linking.

    Broader Roguelike (1001 games) is the expansion target *if* the
    wedge demo lands.

*** Cost expectations (per report)
    - Analysis run (~145 games): $70-145 LLM
    - Cross-genre synthesiser (one row, weekly refresh): ~$5/month
    - Page serving: matview row read + CDN. Negligible.
    - *Total per report: under $200 compute + ~60 hours curation
      (Report #1); ≤ 40 hours for Reports #2+.*

**** Tier 1 products

     |--------------------------------------+--------+-------------------------------------------------|
     | Genre Report — Indie tier            | $49    | Stripe → S3 signed URL PDF (delayed if pre-order) |
     | Genre Report — Studio tier           | $149   | PDF + CSV dataset + 1-yr update access          |

     | =/genre/[slug]/= synthesis page      | $0     | Free forever, SEO-indexed (curated preview)     |
     | All per-game pages                   | $0     | Free forever, SEO-indexed                       |

     All three paid tiers are *fully self-serve* Stripe buttons. No
     contact form, no manual invoicing, no email scoping.

     *Free vs paid shape.* The =/genre/[slug]/= page is a **curated
     preview** (author byline + editorial intro + top-5 friction +
     top-3 wishlist + 3 benchmark cards + churn stat with one-line
     interpretation + 2-row dev priorities teaser). The paid PDF is
     the **full synthesis** (all 10 friction / 10 wishlist / 5 benchmarks
     with full quote sets) plus 15–20 pages of hand-written benchmark
     deep-dives, executive summary, strategic recommendations,
     print-ready design, and (Studio/Publisher) CSV + JSON datasets.
     See =scripts/prompts/monetization-strategy.md= → "Free vs paid"
     for the canonical table. Curation on the free page is
     load-bearing: Google's March 2026 core update demotes
     mass-produced LLM content without named-human editing, and the
     anti-cannibalisation rule is that AI assistants should be able to
     cite the preview but not finish the buyer's question from it.

     Per-game pages (=/games/[appid]/[slug]/=) stay **fully visible,
     full sections, no curation** — commodity-floor parity with
     competitors and the programmatic-SEO breadth layer.
     Cannibalisation risk vs. the paid PDF is zero because the PDF is
     a different artifact shape (cross-genre synthesis, not a per-game
     collection).

** Marketing copy — homepage + reports page

   Rule: never say "AI" in headlines or CTAs. Show the output, not the
   tech.

*** Homepage hero
    - *Primary:* "Deep market reports for Steam game devs." / "Synthesised from every player review across an entire genre. Free previews, paid PDFs from $49."
    - *Alt (dev-targeted for r/gamedev):* "Know your genre before you ship." / "Market reports that read every review of every competitor so you don't have to."

*** Reports page copy
    - Lead with the single most surprising finding from the published report
    - Show the full table of contents (buyers need to know what they get before paying)
    - Include a 3-page sample PDF download (email capture)
    - Three clear tiers with feature comparison; all three self-serve Stripe buttons

*** Phrasing to avoid
    - "AI-synthesised player intelligence" → "Player intelligence distilled from thousands of reviews"
    - "AI-powered review analysis" → "Every review read, every pattern surfaced"
