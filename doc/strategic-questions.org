#+TITLE: High-Value Strategic Questions SteamPulse Will Answer
#+DESCRIPTION: The questions that make SteamPulse useful to indie developers — not just a review aggregator, but a market intelligence tool.

These are the questions that make SteamPulse genuinely useful to indie
developers — not just a review aggregator, but a market intelligence tool.

* "What genre should I concentrate on right now?"
Multi-signal answer combining data we already collect:

- *Supply gap:* Genres with growing review velocity but few new releases = underserved
- *Sentiment quality:* Genres where even mid-tier games score "Very Positive" = forgiving audience
- *Wishlist density:* =player_wishlist= items repeating across many reports = unmet demand
- *Hidden gem ratio:* High % of hidden gems = good games getting buried = opportunity
- *Review velocity trend:* Is the genre growing, peaking, or declining?

Surfaced as an *Opportunity Score per genre* once we have 500+ analyzed games.

Example output:
#+begin_example
Roguelike Deckbuilders: 🔥 High opportunity
— 40 games analyzed, avg "Very Positive"
— Top wishlist: "endless run mode", "more card synergies", "co-op"
— 3 hidden gems under 1k reviews
— Review velocity: growing +18% YoY
#+end_example

* "What features are players looking for in genre X?"
Cross-report synthesis — aggregated from all reports tagged with that genre:

- =player_wishlist= ranked by frequency across all genre games → what doesn't exist yet
- =gameplay_friction= → what to avoid building
- =churn_triggers= → what kills retention in this genre
- =design_strengths= → what the genre's best games do right
- =competitive_context= → the benchmark games players compare everything to

Example output for "Survival":
#+begin_example
Players want: base-building automation, narrative depth, co-op story mode
They hate: hunger/thirst micromanagement, punishing early death loops, empty worlds
They churn at: 5-hour mark when base feels "solved"
Benchmark: they compare everything to Valheim
#+end_example

This is what Chris Zukowski (howtomarketagame.com) does manually — reads
hundreds of reviews to write genre analysis posts. We generate it automatically.

* V1 delivery (pre-chat)
Pre-built genre insight pages: =/genre/survival= shows aggregated wishlist,
top friction points, opportunity score, hidden gems. No chat interface needed.
These pages are also high-value SEO targets (developers Google "survival game
player complaints" and land on us).

* V2 delivery (chat interface)
User types the question in natural language. The chat layer translates NL → SQL
against pre-computed artifacts (matviews + cross-report synthesis matviews +
=reports.report_json=) and answers from the result set. No vector RAG over raw
reviews. Same answer architecture as the V1 pages, just with a different front
door.

See =ARCHITECTURE.org= → "Synthesize, don't retrieve-raw" for the load-bearing
architectural principle and =doc/top-100-questions.org= for the 100 example
questions plus a coverage breakdown.
