* Marketing & indie game strategy (Chris Zukowski / GDC circle)

Foundational marketing voice in the indie space. His manual genre
analysis is the model for SteamPulse's automated cross-genre synthesis
wedge.

- [[https://howtomarketagame.com/][How To Market A Game]] (Chris Zukowski's blog)
- [[https://howtomarketagame.com/about/][About Chris Zukowski]]
- [[https://howtomarketagame.com/blog/][Blog]]
- [[https://howtomarketagame.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/05/Zukowski_60MistakesEbookV1.pdf][60 Game Marketing Mistakes (ebook)]]
- [[https://www.gamedeveloper.com/marketing/super-practical-indie-game-marketing-with-chris-zukowski---gdc-podcast-ep-24][GDC Podcast ep. 24 — Practical indie marketing]]
- [[https://www.gamedeveloper.com/marketing/game-developer-podcast-36-indie-marketing-advice-from-chris-zukowski][Game Developer Podcast ep. 34]]
- [[https://unitycodemonkey.com/howtomarketagame.php][Code Monkey — How to Market a Game feat. Chris Zukowski]]
- [[https://80.lv/articles/5-tips-strategies-for-marketing-indie-games][80.lv — 5 tips & strategies]]

Key takeaways applied to launch strategy:
- Community building > press coverage (Valheim, Tiny Glade examples)
- Genres that outperform: horror, "crafty building strategy simulation-y games"
- Demos are the post-2020 meta; Steam page optimisation matters more than press
- Email lists convert (Tiny Glade built 25k before launch)
- Stair-step discount strategy; avoid deep early discounts
